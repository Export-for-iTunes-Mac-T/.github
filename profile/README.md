## 📄 Overview

<a href="#">Export for iTunes</a> solves the extraction problem that Apple's music ecosystem creates: getting your own music library out of iTunes or Apple Music and onto an external drive, NAS, or backup destination in an organized way that preserves the playlist structure, metadata, and folder organization you have built over years.

The <a href="#">playlist-based export system</a> copies music organized by playlist hierarchy rather than dumping everything into a single flat folder. Your playlists become folders, nested playlists become nested folders, and every track lands where it belongs relative to the playlist structure that reflects how you organize your music. <a href="#">Complete metadata preservation</a> carries ratings, play counts, last played dates, comments, artwork, and all other tag data from the library into the exported files so the copy is as rich as the original.

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://static.macupdate.com/screenshots/331446/m/export-for-itunes-screenshot.png?v=1656510750" alt="Export for iTunes screenshot"/>

<a href="#">Selective export options</a> export specific playlists, albums, artists, or the entire library depending on what the current operation requires — a full library backup, a playlist for a road trip drive, or a selection for a specific device. <a href="#">Duplicate handling</a> identifies and manages cases where the same track appears in multiple playlists, offering to copy once and create references or copy separately for each playlist depending on the destination's intended use.

The <a href="#">external drive optimization</a> structures exports for direct use on portable drives, NAS devices, and media servers — organizing files in ways that media players and network storage browsers can read and navigate without additional reorganization. <a href="#">Export progress reporting</a> shows transfer status, identifies any files that could not be copied due to DRM or missing source files, and produces a completion report of what was and was not exported.

## 🤔 FAQ

<details>
<summary>Can Export for iTunes export Apple Music streaming tracks?</summary>

Only tracks that are locally stored on the Mac can be exported. Streaming-only tracks from Apple Music that have not been downloaded to the device cannot be exported as the audio data is not locally available.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does exporting preserve playlist structure as folders?</summary>

Yes. The export creates a folder hierarchy matching your playlist and nested playlist organization, with each playlist becoming a folder containing the tracks assigned to it.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I export only specific playlists rather than the entire library?</summary>

Yes. Selective export allows choosing specific playlists, albums, artists, or any combination for export rather than requiring a full library export every time.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does export work with DRM-protected tracks?</summary>

Tracks protected by DRM that cannot be played without authorization cannot be exported. Export for iTunes identifies these tracks in its completion report so you know which files are affected.

</details>
